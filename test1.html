Executive Summary

First impressions are critical. As the initial section of the proposal, the executive summary should do two things well: Encapsulate what the rest of the document will expound on and create a compelling reason for your potential client to accept your proposal. In one to two paragraphs, try to establish your company's credibility, your understanding of the client's needs, and how your company can fulfill them. How experienced is your company? Which services will best address your client's requirements? What value and concrete benefits can they expect if they choose to work with you?
